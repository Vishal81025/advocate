For your first meeting, bring any relevant documents — contracts, court notices, FIR copies, property papers, or correspondence. Bring a valid government-issued ID and any prior lawyer records. The more information you share, the better we can advise you.

We offer a free initial consultation of up to 30 minutes for all new clients. For complex matters requiring detailed review, a nominal consultation fee may apply and will be communicated clearly beforehand.

Simple cases may resolve in 6–18 months. Complex property or High Court matters can take 2–5 years. We always explore faster options like mediation, settlements, and interim relief applications to protect your interests.

Absolutely. Everything you share — in person, by phone, or email — is strictly protected by attorney-client privilege under the Advocates Act 1961 and the Indian Evidence Act. We never disclose your information without written consent.

For bailable offences, bail is a right obtainable at the police station. For non-bailable offences, we file before the Magistrate or Sessions Court. We are available 24/7 for urgent bail matters and have secured same-day bail in many cases.

Criminal cases are filed by the state for offences under the IPC — outcomes include imprisonment, fines, or acquittal. Civil cases are disputes between private parties over rights, property, contracts, or money — the remedy is compensation or an injunction, not imprisonment.

Mutual consent divorce (Section 13B, Hindu Marriage Act) requires only two court appearances and typically completes in 6–18 months. If contested, a full trial is required. We always recommend mediation first to save time and emotional stress.
